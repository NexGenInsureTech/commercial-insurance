The Strategic Pivot: From High Cost to High Value

The Core Problem: Our current broker-led channel suffers from high commission outages (up to 16.5%), which significantly erodes profitability and makes large-scale growth unsustainable. A broker-led model cannot profitably deliver a ₹50 Crore portfolio.

The Strategic Solution: We will build a "pure commercial" direct sales channel. This pivot shifts costs from high variable commissions to manageable fixed salaries and performance incentives, fundamentally improving profitability per policy and enabling scalable growth.

Key Benefits:

  • Enhanced Profitability: Drastically reduce channel costs and retain more premium.
  • Direct Customer Ownership: Build long-term relationships, enabling cross-selling and improving retention.
  • Brand Control: Communicate our value proposition directly, differentiating on expertise, not just price.

The First Year: From Foundation to Execution

Goal: Build capability and consistency before market entry.

  • Complete 5-day intensive training.
  • Master CRM for activity logging and pipeline management.
  • Role-play client scenarios to internalize the sales playbook.
  • Build initial prospect lists for target sectors.
Phase 2: Active Selling & Refining (Wks 13-40)

Goal: Engage the market, gather feedback, and refine the process.

  • Execute weekly activity targets: 50 calls, 5 meetings, 2 proposals.
  • Attend mandatory weekly team huddles to review calls and share learnings.
  • Provide direct feedback to management on playbook effectiveness.
Phase 3: Consistent Execution (Wks 41-52)

Goal: Drive predictable results by scaling the validated playbook.

  • Non-negotiable adherence to weekly activity KPIs.
  • Focus shifts from process refinement to conversion rate optimization.
  • Consistently meet and exceed monthly GWP targets.

Product Sales Playbooks

The "Land" Playbook: Cyber Liability

Your spearhead for entering new accounts. This is a conversation about survival in the digital age.

Why?The risk is tangible, growing, and driven by the DPDP Act. A data breach is an existential threat to an MSME.
Whom?Founder/CEO/CFO of tech-enabled businesses in IT/ITeS and D2C sectors, primarily in Mumbai & Bengaluru.
What? (The Pitch)"We provide Balance Sheet protection. Our policy offers the capital to survive a major cyber event, covering fines, legal fees, and reputational damage."
How? (Discovery Qs)"How do you store customer data? What's your response plan for a ransomware attack? Can your P&L absorb a ₹50 Lakh fine tomorrow?"
The "Must Buy" Argument:

For any business in the IT or D2C sector, data is the lifeblood. The DPDP Act has turned data protection from a best practice into a legal mandate with severe financial penalties. Operating without Cyber Insurance is no longer a calculated risk; it's a direct threat to the company's solvency and the personal liability of its directors.

The "Expand" Playbook: D&O and E&O

Leverage the trust from the Cyber sale to address management and professional risks, significantly increasing client value.

Why?To protect the personal assets of leaders (D&O) and safeguard against service delivery failures (E&O).
Whom?Existing clients who have bought Cyber. Target funded startups for D&O and all service-based businesses for E&O.
What? (The Pitch)"Now that we've protected the company's data, let's protect its leaders and its reputation. D&O shields your personal wealth from lawsuits, while E&O covers you if a client claims your service caused them a financial loss."
How? (Pivot Qs)"If the data breach we discussed led to a service outage, could your clients sue you for their losses (E&O)? Would your investors hold you personally responsible (D&O)?"
The "Must Buy" Argument:

For funded startups, D&O is non-negotiable to attract investors and board members. For any IT/ITeS firm, E&O is essential, as their client contracts almost certainly contain liability clauses for service failures. These policies are not upsells; they are fundamental components of a robust risk management framework for any serious business.
