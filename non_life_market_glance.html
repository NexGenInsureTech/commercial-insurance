Risk Management as a Service (RaaS): Product Catalog

A deep dive into the RaaS product construct, transforming the insurer's role from a simple vendor to a strategic risk partner for high-value SMEs.

Business Shield: Manufacturing+

1. Risk Mapping

  • Operational: Machinery breakdown, fire at premises, supply chain disruption, skilled labor accidents.
  • Financial: Business interruption costs, damage to high-value stock, product recall expenses.
  • Liability: Defective product claims (product liability), workplace accidents (workmen's compensation).

2. Product Construct

  • Core Covers: Standard Fire & Special Perils (IAR Policy), Machinery Breakdown, Burglary.
  • Value-Add Covers: Business Interruption (following fire/machinery breakdown), Product Liability, Public Liability.
  • Key Exclusions: Normal wear & tear, willful negligence, war & terrorism (can be covered as add-on).

3. Underwriting & Actuarial Notes

▸

Underwriting: Focus on quality of machinery (age, maintenance records), fire safety installations (sprinklers, alarms), and supply chain resilience. A physical risk survey is mandatory for sums insured above ₹50 Cr.
Actuarial: Pricing to be loaded based on industry type (e.g., chemical vs. assembly), claims history (3-year loss ratio), and quality of risk management practices observed during the survey.

Business Shield: TechGuard+

1. Risk Mapping

  • Operational: System downtime, data breach, loss of critical data, project delivery failure.
  • Financial: Costs of data restoration, regulatory fines (e.g., GDPR, DPDP Act), reputational damage, contractual penalties.
  • Liability: Errors & Omissions (E&O) leading to client financial loss, intellectual property infringement, Directors & Officers (D&O) liability.

2. Product Construct

  • Core Covers: Professional Indemnity (E&O), Cyber Insurance (first-party & third-party).
  • Value-Add Covers: Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability, Commercial General Liability, Office Package Policy (for assets).
  • Key Exclusions: Deliberate fraudulent acts, prior known circumstances, infrastructure failure of third-party vendors (e.g., cloud provider outage).

3. Underwriting & Actuarial Notes

▸

Underwriting: Scrutinize contractual agreements with clients, cybersecurity posture (MFA, encryption, backup policy), and employee training protocols. A self-assessment cybersecurity questionnaire is mandatory.
Actuarial: Pricing heavily influenced by nature of service (e.g., FinTech vs. BPO), geographical exposure (USA/EU clients attract higher loading), and limits of liability requested. Deductibles structured to encourage good risk behavior.

Business Shield: GlobalTrade+

1. Risk Mapping

  • Operational: Damage to goods in transit (sea/air/land), port delays, customs issues, cargo theft.
  • Financial: Loss of revenue from damaged goods, currency fluctuation losses, non-payment by overseas buyer (trade credit risk).
  • Liability: Product rejection at destination due to damage, liability to shipping lines or port authorities.

2. Product Construct

  • Core Covers: Marine Cargo Insurance (Open Policy), Fire & Burglary for stock at warehouse.
  • Value-Add Covers: Trade Credit Insurance (against buyer default), Product Rejection Insurance, Seller's Interest Insurance.
  • Key Exclusions: Inherent vice of cargo, improper packaging, losses due to sanctions or trade embargoes.

3. Underwriting & Actuarial Notes

▸

Underwriting: Assess nature of goods (perishable vs. durable), trade routes (high-risk piracy areas), packaging standards, and buyer's creditworthiness (for trade credit). Past transit loss history is a key factor.
Actuarial: Rates vary significantly based on conveyance type (air is cheaper than sea), destination country risk profile, and coverage clauses (e.g., ICC-A vs. ICC-C). Volume discounts available for high-turnover exporters under an open policy structure.
